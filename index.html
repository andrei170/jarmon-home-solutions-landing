EV & Electrical FAQ

Good questions, straight answers

Can I still claim the federal 30C tax credit for a home EV charger?+
The federal 30C tax credit for residential EV charging equipment is currently scheduled to expire June 30, 2026, so the install must be complete before that date to be claimed on a 2026 return. Eligibility decisions stay with you and your accountant. We provide the documentation you need to support the claim.
How much does an EV charger install cost in Cincinnati?+
It depends on the distance from your panel, whether a panel upgrade is needed, and the charger model. We assess quickly and give a clear, upfront quote before any work starts.
How long does an EV charger installation take?+
A straightforward Level 2 install where no panel upgrade is needed usually finishes in a single visit of a few hours. If your panel needs an upgrade, plan on an additional visit, which we can schedule the same week so the full job still wraps fast.
Do I need a panel upgrade for an EV charger?+
Sometimes. A Level 2 charger draws real power, so older or near-capacity panels may need an upgrade. We check first and tell you straight before any work begins.
Do I need a permit for an EV charger in Cincinnati?+
Yes. Cincinnati and the surrounding jurisdictions require an electrical permit for a Level 2 EV charger install, and inspection after the work is complete. We handle the permit, the install, and the inspection coordination as part of the job.
Will an EV charger affect my home insurance?+
A code-correct, permitted, professionally installed charger generally does not raise homeowners insurance. A DIY or unpermitted install can, and may also create issues at the time of a claim. Confirm specifics with your insurance provider.
Are you licensed and insured?+
Yes. We are a licensed electrician and general contractor registered with the City of Cincinnati and the State of Ohio, working to NEC code and OSHA standards.
